" Mary Oliver is saving my life, " Paul Chowder, the tille character of Nicholson Baker's novel"The Anthologist" scrawls in the margins of Oliver's"New and Selected Poems,Volume One"

A struggling poet, Chowder is suffering from a severe case of writer's block. His girlfrend, with whom he's lived for eight years, has just left him, ostensibly because he has been unable to write the long-overdue inroduction to a poetry antholory that he has putting together. For solace and inspiration, he tuens to poets who have been his touchstones--Louise Bogan, Thedore Roethke, Sara Teasdale--before discovering Oliver. In her work, He find consolation: " I immedialy felt more sure of hhat i was doing." Of her poems, he says, "They're simple. Andyet each has something."
I don't know exactly what a prayer is
I do know how to pay attention, how to fall down

how to be idle and blessed, how to stroll through the fields,
